# Brand Identity: LumaLingua

## 1. Best Name

### LumaLingua

**LumaLingua** is the recommended master brand: a premium, memorable name that feels like a place, a method, and a modern SaaS product at once.

**Why it works**

- **Psychology:** “Luma” suggests light, clarity, discovery, and visual understanding; “Lingua” immediately signals language without tying the product to German.
- **Emotion:** Warm curiosity, guided exploration, confidence, and the feeling of language becoming visible.
- **Immersion fit:** The name naturally supports a world of illustrated scenes where meaning is illuminated through image, sound, and interaction.
- **Global scalability:** Easy to pronounce across English, Portuguese, Spanish, French, and German; adaptable to LumaLingua German, LumaLingua Spanish, LumaLingua Kids, and LumaLingua Tutor.
- **Premium signal:** Soft vowels, balanced rhythm, and a polished tech-learning feel make it credible at a €10–20/month subscription price.

## 4. Brand Story

LumaLingua turns language learning into a world you can enter.

Instead of memorizing translated word lists, learners explore illustrated scenes, tap objects, hear native pronunciation, and understand meaning through context.

The product exists because language is not best learned as a spreadsheet; it is learned as an environment full of people, places, sounds, and patterns.

Every scene is designed to make German feel intuitive before it feels analytical.

Like a premium storybook powered by an intelligent learning engine, LumaLingua helps users build comprehension naturally through repeated visual discovery.

It feels calm, playful, and polished—built for adults who want progress, families who want delight, and future learners across any language.

## 5. Visual Identity Guidelines

### Color System

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Premium foundation | Deep Graphite | `#111113` | App chrome, navigation, hero backgrounds, premium framing. |
| Elevated surface | Charcoal Slate | `#1B1C20` | Cards, modals, immersive panels, lesson surfaces. |
| Primary energy | Luma Red | `#C7352E` | Calls to action, selected objects, key progress moments. |
| Reward accent | Warm Gold | `#F2B84B` | Completion states, streaks, highlights, celebratory feedback. |
| Soft learning glow | Honey Light | `#FFE2A3` | Gentle illumination, object halos, hover states, onboarding warmth. |
| Calm canvas | Porcelain Mist | `#F7F3EA` | Reading surfaces, empty states, premium contrast against dark UI. |
| Success | Alpine Green | `#3A8F6A` | Correct answers, mastery feedback, progress confirmations. |

### Palette Principles

- Use the German flag as inspiration, not as literal nationalism: graphite replaces black, red becomes richer and more elegant, yellow becomes golden and intelligent.
- Keep dark surfaces matte and calm; avoid pure black except for typography or deep contrast.
- Use gold sparingly as a reward color so it feels earned, not decorative.
- Use red for agency and attention, especially tap targets and moments of discovery.

### Typography Direction

- **Primary direction:** A modern humanist sans-serif with warmth and high legibility, such as **Aptos**, **Inter**, **Söhne**, **Avenir Next**, or **Geist**.
- **Personality:** Clean like Apple, friendly like Headspace, structured enough for SaaS credibility.
- **Headlines:** Rounded confidence, generous letter spacing, short declarative phrasing.
- **Body:** High x-height, excellent diacritics, comfortable reading for multilingual learners.
- **UI labels:** Medium weight, never overly playful, optimized for quick recognition during interaction.

### Logo Direction

- Create a simple luminous portal, speech bubble, or illustrated window mark that suggests entering a language world.
- The icon can combine a small golden glow with a red discovery point inside a graphite frame.
- Avoid flags, owls, graduation caps, pencils, books, or childish mascots as the primary identity device.
- The logo should work as a premium app icon, favicon, lesson badge, and AI tutor avatar foundation.

### UI Tone

- Calm, immersive, minimal, and expressive.
- Interfaces should step back so scenes feel like the product’s main stage.
- Use spacious cards, soft shadows, cinematic transitions, and object-focused highlights.
- Copy should be short, encouraging, and sensory: “Tap what you hear,” “Find the red car,” “Listen again,” “You discovered 6 new words.”

### Motion Style

- Soft object pulses on hover or tap, like an item gently coming alive.
- Fluid scene transitions with slight parallax to reinforce depth and exploration.
- Pronunciation feedback should feel instant and tactile: a small glow, ripple, or sound wave.
- Completion animations should be restrained and premium: warm gold bloom, subtle confetti dust, or progress ring fill.
- AI tutor mode can use a breathing glow or conversational orb, not a cartoon mascot.

## 6. Expansion Architecture

- **Language lines:** LumaLingua German, LumaLingua Spanish, LumaLingua French, LumaLingua English.
- **Audience modes:** LumaLingua Kids, LumaLingua Family, LumaLingua Classroom.
- **AI modes:** LumaLingua Tutor, LumaLingua Guide, LumaLingua Conversation.
- **Content worlds:** LumaLingua City, LumaLingua Home, LumaLingua Market, LumaLingua Stories.

The name should remain the parent world while languages, modes, and scenes become destinations inside the broader LumaLingua ecosystem.
